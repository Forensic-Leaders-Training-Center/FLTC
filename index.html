What Makes FLTC Different?

Affordability

We're on a mission to democratize leadership development with affordable programs, courses, and coaching, all without sacrificing quality. You shouldn't have to pay for executive-level training that doesn't fit your position. We want to provide quality service that meets you where you are.

Flexibility

We know crime scenes don't occur on a regular schedule, and rush cases can come into the lab at any time. That's why we're offering a flexible and comprehensive leadership development solution that's easily accessible at any time and from anywhere in the world.

Community

You are part of a unique professional community that's doing challenging work. We want to bring you and like-minded peers together with a network that is welcoming and supportive.

Personalization

You are a unique person, with individual needs, goals, and challenges at work. That's why we're offering you the chance to work with a coach who can guide you through those tough times and situations that are uniquely yours.

Meet Our Coaches

Internally Guided Leadership

by Kayla Sweet, MA
FLTC Leadership & Burnout Resilience Coach

Stop sacrificing yourself for your work. Living with chronic stress and experiencing life through a fog of exhaustion sets you up to fail professionally and personally. Luckily, there's a better way. You can achieve professional excellence while cultivating a life that you love. If you are serious about leading others and living a life that lights you up, you must start within yourself.

I'm Kayla, and I'll help you recover from burnout and create a life of passion, presence, and purpose.

I am a Leadership & Burnout Resilience Coach at FLTC. In addition, I work as a Talent Acquisition Consultant with The Bowen Center. I have completed academic and professional leadership training and served as a mentor/trainer in leadership development programs. My BA is in Psychology, and my MA is in Consciousness and Transformative Studies. I specialize in Transformational Leadership and Consciousness and Healing. I received the 2018 Library Prize for Undergraduate Research for my paper entitled Potential of Mindfulness and Art Therapy for Emergency First Responders. I was also the recipient of the 2017/2018 James R. Haines Award for Research in Psychology. My MA thesis explores Internally Guided Leadership and is a theoretical synthesis of leadership and psychological theories with applications in the field of leadership.

Vital Resilience

by Patrick Nottingham, MA, CReC
FLTC Resilience Coach

I geek out on people and teams maximizing efficiency, improving process, connecting the dots to the short and long term vision, turning light bulb moments into executed momentum, and realizing the value in their work and each other all while keeping strategy and empathy at the forefront.

Experienced entrepreneur and customer success savant with a demonstrated history in technology, organizational wellness, mental health, law enforcement, fitness, and travel & tourism industries. Diverse skillsets in CS/CX, training and education, coaching and consultation, resiliency, problem identification and resolution, writing and public speaking, advocacy and empowerment, process optimization, and project management.

Over the past 12 years, I have worked with thousands of people from all walks of life dealing with chaos, disconnection, burnout, and stress. That experience has helped me break down the "big picture" of stress into bite sized steps for action and change.

It’s been an education in itself, witnessing the amazing power people have to change their own lives. And it’s taught me a tremendous amount about what matters with resiliency and what doesn’t. (Plot twist: a lot of the things I used to care about in coaching, don’t matter much anymore. And things I used to not care very much about are now my top priorities.)

Sherrie Gilbert-Ramsey, MS

FLTC Crisis Leadership & Management Coach

Sherrie G. is a Leadership Coach, Self-Discovery Coach and Crisis Management Coach who has been a therapist, life coach, corporate trainer, and consultant to clients of all ages and walks of life. She has worked in Public Schools, DFCS, Intensive Family Intervention, CORE agencies, Law Enforcement, Corrections, and is a United States Army Veteran.

She has over 24 years of experience in coaching, consulting, and crisis management. She is a Crisis Intervention Team Instructor, Crisis Prevention Specialist and Certified Crisis Responder. She is also Georgia POST Certified Instructor, Certified Hostage Negotiator, Shoplifting and Theft Addiction Specialist, Nationally Certified Anger Management Specialist, Supervised Visitation Provider, Disaster Assistance and Response Specialist and a Disruptive Event Management Specialist.

With over two decades of experience working in some of the most exciting and demanding business environments, Sherrie is able to bring practical real-world experience to her coaching, mentoring and speaking. It’s this fusion of first-hand experience, personal stories, and humor that enable her to connect so deeply with her audiences and create such inspiring and transformational talks. Sherrie has led law enforcement officers, worked for high profile executives, worked high profile events, and has had the opportunity to work with the most senior figures in the corporate and entertainment world. Her unique skill set sets her apart from everyone in the industry.

Brian Reinarz, MS

FLTC Forensic Leadership Coach

I am an LAPD criminalist with over 21 years of experience in a variety of forensic disciplines to include Forensic Alcohol Analysis, Controlled Substances (drug analysis), Biology Screening, Firearms Analysis, and Crime Scene Response. Prior to LAPD (5 years), I was a Criminalist with the California Department of Justice in Riverside and Berkeley California for 16.5 years.

At LAPD, I am currently a technical lead in the Firearm Analysis Unit (FAU) and the primary trainer for firearm examination and crime scene field response. I also serve as a mentor to younger examiners and field responders.

I was the instructor/lecturer/professor of "Crime Scene Management" and "Contemporary Issues in Forensic Science" at California State University, Los Angeles from 2018 to 2020, when COVID shut down much of the instruction. I fully expect to resume my position soon with the recent resumption of in-person instruction. Prior to Cal State, I was the instructor of "Introduction to Criminalistics" at Riverside Community College for approximately 6 years.

I am a United States Air Force veteran (22 years total). I was an active duty Security Policeman for 9 years and a reserve Aerospace Medic for 13 years.

Stephen Lu, EMBA, SHRM-CP

FLTC Executive Director & Forensic Strategy Development Coach

I am a social entrepreneur focused on addressing social and leadership challenges through collaborative, innovative communication and technology solutions. I leverage strong analytical and interpersonal skills to drive positive change in local communities and organizations.

I have 17 years of extensive forensic experience and training, including Forensic Biology, Crime Scene and Shooting Incident Investigation and Reconstruction, and Firearms Analysis. Throughout my forensics career, I have worked for the California Department of Justice, the Arizona Department of Public Safety, the National Forensic Science Technology Center, and the San Diego County Sheriff's Department. I have testified as a qualified expert witness in courts in Arizona and California and in front of the U.S. District Court for the Central District of California. I have served as the Regional Director South and Lead Webmaster for the California Association of Criminalists. I currently serve as a member of the San Diego County Sheriff's LGBTQ+ Advisory Council, and am a graduate of the 2023 FBI San Diego Citizens' Academy.

Before my career in forensics, I worked for six years as a published research scientist. I studied protease regulation in the Yellow Fever mosquito, Aedes aegypti, at the University of Arizona Department of Biochemistry. I also completed an internship at the National Institutes of Health/NIDDK, studying neutral lipid trafficking in Niemann-Pick Type C disease. In September 2021, I became the Executive Director of A Lasting Strength, a non-profit organization dedicated to supporting and improving the lives of people with Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS). I also serve as a community member of the Patient and Family Advisory Council at UC San Diego Health.

Earlier this year, I earned professional certification with the Society for Human Resource Management. In addition, I expect to earn my Executive MBA from Quantic School of Business & Technology later this year. Through this program, I've developed a fascination with strategic planning and entrepreneurship, and I have gained specialized training in these areas.

Flexibility in Leadership. Trust through Consistency.
  • Flexibility
    We cultivate a culture of adaptability and flexibility in our course offerings and services, recognizing that individual needs may vary. The ability to adjust quickly is an essential leadership skill.
  • Leadership
    Effective leadership encompasses more than superior people management or having all the answers. We aim to help our members develop self-awareness and a comprehensive skillset to become well-rounded leaders.
  • Trust
    The relationships between forensic practitioners, the criminal justice community, and law enforcement require robust public trust. Effective leadership is crucial to a forensic laboratory's success, as trust is integral to the efficacy of the criminal justice process, impacting citizens' lives and well-being.
  • Consistency
    Consistency between words and actions is a hallmark of authentic and integral leadership, demonstrating credibility and reliability to others.
